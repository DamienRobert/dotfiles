Welcome

Welcome to my professional website. I am an INRIA researcher in the LFANT team with Andreas Enge.

Previously, I was an engineer researcher at Microsoft Research (MSR) in the cryptography group of Kristin Lauter at Redmond (USA).

I work in the field of applications of abelian varieties in public key cryptography. My main subject of interest are Jacobians of hyperelliptic curves (and more specifically genus 2 curves), computing isogenies and point counting, and also arithmetic with theta functions and class polynomials generation. I worked on my PhD thesis under the supervision of Guillaume Hanrot in the Caramel team at Nancy.

I maintain a list of my publications, and a list of softwares.

You can also contact me, look at some more informations on me or take a peek at my Curriculum Vitae.
